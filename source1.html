Super Mario

Super Mario[a] is a platform game series created by Nintendo based on Mario.
Alternatively called the Super Mario Bros.[b] series or simply the Mario[c] series,
it is the central series of the greater Mario franchise. At least one Super Mario game
has been released for every major Nintendo video game console. Many Super Mario
video games are on non-Nintendo platforms.[1] There are twenty-one similar games
and one cross-series game that may or may not be included as part of the series.
The Super Mario games are typically in the fictional Mushroom Kingdom with Mario as
the player character. He is often joined by his brother, Luigi, and occasionally by
other members of the Mario cast. As a platform game, the player runs and jumps across
platforms and atop enemies in themed levels. The games have simple plots, typically with
Mario rescuing the kidnapped Princess Peach from the primary antagonist, Bowser.
The first game in the series, Super Mario Bros., released for the Nintendo Entertainment
System (NES) in 1985, established the series' gameplay concepts and elements.
These include a multitude of power-ups and items that give Mario special powers
such as fireball-throwing and size-changing into both giant and miniature sizes.[2]
